# Pork Loin on a Kamado (Juicy, Glazed Roast)
*Based on extracted techniques from two YouTube videos (see **Referenced videos** at bottom).*  
Core method: **brine for moisture → smoke for flavor → finish hot with a sweet glaze**.

---

## 1) Quick plan (actionable overview)
1. **Brine** (overnight preferred; 4+ hours works).
2. **Dry + season** (pat dry; apply rub).
3. **Smoke low** until **110–120°F internal**.
4. **Crank the heat + glaze** in layers until **140–145°F internal**.
5. **Rest 10–15 min**, slice.

---

## 2) Kamado setup (two-zone, smoke then hot finish)
### Charcoal + smoke wood
- Load lump charcoal (enough for a full cook). Light a small starter area (kamado equivalent of “minion” style: mostly unlit fuel with a small lit portion).
- Add **3–5 chunks** of a mild fruit wood (e.g., **cherry**). Add early so smoke is **clean** before the meat goes on.

### Configuration
- **Heat deflector in** (indirect) for the smoking phase.
- Drip pan optional.
- Keep humidity simple (no need to add water just to “make it moist”; technique emphasized control + brine).

### Temps (pit)
- **Stage 1 (smoke): 225–250°F** (primary) *or* **275–300°F** (secondary approach).
- **Stage 2 (glaze finish): 300–400°F** (primary). On a kamado, this is: open vents more and let it climb, still indirect if possible (or brief direct carefully at the very end if you know your cooker).

---

## 3) Brine (choose one)
### Option A: Simple moisture brine (Primary)
- **¼ cup salt per quart of water**
- Example: **2 quarts water + ½ cup salt**
- **Time:** overnight ideal; **4 hours** still helps.

### Option B: 5% holiday brine (Secondary)
- **5% salt** in water (by weight).
- Add aromatics (their style): brown sugar, garlic, warm spices, peppercorns, herbs, plus **apple cider vinegar**.
- **Time:** overnight.

After brine: rinse optional; always **pat very dry** before seasoning.

---

## 4) Seasoning (rub)
### Primary rub (simple pantry blend)
- ~**½ tbsp salt** (adjust if brined aggressively)
- **2 tbsp smoked paprika**
- **2 tsp black pepper**
- **1 tsp garlic powder**
- **1 tsp ground coriander**
- **½ tsp ground cumin**
- Optional: lemon zest powder

Technique note (Primary): **“Pat it in. Don’t rub it.”**

### Secondary quick seasoning
- **Black pepper + granulated garlic** (simple works; glaze carries a lot of flavor).

---

## 5) Cooking targets (internal temp)
- **Smoke phase:** cook until **110–120°F internal**.
- **Finish phase (glaze):** take to **140–145°F internal**, then rest.

> Both videos land around **140–145°F** for slicing (with rest). Avoid pushing lean loin much higher or it dries out.

---

## 6) Estimated time (rules of thumb)
Times vary by loin thickness, starting temp, and pit temp.
- At **225–250°F:** often **~2 to 3.5 hours** total for a typical pork loin roast (plus rest), with the last portion faster once you raise pit temp.
- At **275–300°F:** often **~1.5 to 2.5 hours** total.

Best practice: cook to **thermometer temps**, not the clock.

---

## 7) Glaze + finish (key technique notes)
### Primary-style spicy lime-honey glaze (concept)
- Honey + lime juice/zest + apple cider vinegar + salt (optional whole jalapeños), **reduce**, then **mount with ~1 tbsp butter** for shine.

### Secondary-style honey bourbon holiday glaze (concept)
- Honey + Dijon + brown sugar + apple juice; simmer, add bourbon and cook off; finish with **cold butter**.

### How to glaze on a kamado
1. When loin reaches **110–120°F internal**, open vents to raise pit to **300–400°F**.
2. **Brush a thin coat** of glaze.
3. Let it set 5–10 minutes, then repeat **2–4 light layers**.
4. Watch carefully: **sugar burns fast** at higher heat.

Optional: if you want extra caramelization, do a *very brief* direct exposure at the end (seconds to a minute per side), but only if you can control flare-ups.

---

## 8) Rest + slice
- Rest **10–15 minutes**.
- Slice across the grain into medallions.

Leftover tip from Primary: slices pan-sear well in oil + butter.

# Kamado Pork Loin Roast — Step-by-Step

## What Chuds uses for the dry brine (overnight)
1. **Set up the roast:** Place the pork loin roast on a **rack** over a sheet pan.
2. **Dry brine seasoning:** Apply
   - **Kosher salt**
   - **16-mesh black pepper**
   - **Garlic powder**
3. **Air-dry uncovered:** Refrigerate **uncovered overnight** (still on the rack).

## Full cook (smoke + finish)

### 1) Set up the Kamado for indirect smoking
1. Configure the grill for **indirect heat** (heat deflector/platesetter in place).
2. Add smoking wood if desired.
3. Stabilize at a **low smoking temperature** (typical “low and slow” range).

### 2) Indirect smoke to 110–120°F internal
1. Put the pork loin on the grill **indirect**.
2. Smoke until the **internal temperature reaches 110–120°F**.

### 3) Ramp the pit to 325–375°F to finish
1. Open vents (and adjust as needed) to raise the Kamado to **325–375°F**.
2. Continue cooking until the roast reaches **140–145°F internal**.

### 4) Pull and rest
1. Remove the roast at **140–145°F** internal.
2. Rest **20–30 minutes** before slicing.

### 5) Optional: butter wrap during the rest
- Optionally **wrap with butter** (or wrap and add butter) while resting for extra richness.

---

## Notes
- Target doneness and carryover cooking can vary; resting helps the roast finish gently and retain juices.
